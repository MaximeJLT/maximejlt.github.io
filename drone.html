Running real-time AI perception onboard a drone

A solo project: build a quadcopter from a kit and put a real AI brain on it — an NVIDIA Jetson that detects and tracks objects live, in flight. The end-to-end proof that I can take embedded machine learning all the way onto real flying hardware.

Why I'm building it

My team projects proved the software; this one proves I can own the entire chain myself — from screwing the frame together to deploying a neural network that runs onboard and influences how the aircraft flies.

It's the project that ties everything together: perception, embedded deployment, and real hardware integration — the exact profile the autonomous-systems industry is looking for.

How it works

A small AI computer rides on the drone and does the thinking in real time:

See
Onboard camera
Think
Jetson — YOLO + ByteTrack
Speed up
TensorRT
Talk
MAVLink
Fly
Pixhawk

In plain terms: the camera feeds the Jetson, which detects and tracks targets with a neural network — sped up with TensorRT so it runs fast enough on a small embedded chip. The Jetson then talks to the flight controller (Pixhawk) to influence the flight. In the air, everything is powered from the drone's own battery through a step-down converter.

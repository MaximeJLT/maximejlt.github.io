Projects

Ising Model – Monte Carlo Simulations
Simulated the 2D Ising model using the Metropolis algorithm to explore phase transitions. Analyzed the behavior of magnetic spins on a lattice, computed magnetization, and visualized cluster formation depending on temperature.
Freely Jointed Chain – Polymer Simulation
Implemented a random walk algorithm to simulate a polymer chain in 3D, where each segment is free to orient inside a unit sphere. Studied conformational entropy and probability distributions of end-to-end distances.
Cohesion Energy – Graphene Potential
Modeled atomic interactions in a 2D graphene lattice using a Lennard-Jones potential. Calculated potential energy landscapes at various adsorption sites (Top, Bridge, Hole), and exported XYZ files for visualization.
Random Walkers – Diffusion Process
Simulated the movement of thousands of particles undergoing random motion in 2D. Analyzed their spatial distribution over time, computed mean squared displacement, and confirmed the diffusion law.
